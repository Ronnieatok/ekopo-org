Volunteer in Kenya

Join the movement

We need
volunteers
in ASAL Kenya

Ekopo works on the ground in Turkana, Kajiado, Mandera and Garissa — the driest, most underserved corners of Kenya. We need hands-on volunteers, corporate partners, and community champions who believe every child deserves clean water and a nutritious meal.

Whether you can donate two weeks or two years, your time transforms lives. Our volunteers report that it's one of the most meaningful things they've ever done.

What we do

01
Safe Drinking Water

Drilling boreholes, installing solar-powered pumps and purification units in Turkana, Kajiado and Mandera counties.

02
Cup of Uji

Fortified porridge served daily in schools and community centres — fighting malnutrition one bowl at a time across drought-hit counties.

03
Health & Hygiene

Sanitation training, soap distribution and deworming campaigns keeping children healthy enough to learn and grow.

04
School Meals

Ensuring no child must learn on an empty stomach — a fed child is one who can dream, study and build a future.

Our track record

What have we done
with your help?

With your support, Ekopo has delivered clean water to 12,000+ children and served over 2 million cups of uji in Turkana, Kajiado and Mandera counties since 2019.

Every borehole we drill transforms an entire community. Children who once walked six hours for muddy water now have time to attend school and simply be children.

Our daily uji programme has reversed acute malnutrition in 14 partner schools — attendance has risen 38% since we started. None of this is possible without you.

Success
Stories:

Real children, real change. Three lives transformed through clean water, daily uji, and the belief that every child deserves better.

Turkana County
Akai
9 Years · Clean Water Recipient

Akai used to walk six hours each day for muddy water. Now, thanks to Ekopo's borehole, clean water flows 200 metres from her home. She is back in school and thriving.

Kajiado County
Lekuta
7 Years · Uji Programme Graduate

Severely malnourished after two failed rainy seasons, Lekuta had stopped attending school. After Ekopo's daily uji programme his weight recovered in three months. He hasn't missed class since.

Garissa County
Aisha
11 Years · Emergency Relief

During the 2022 drought, Aisha's family faced a crisis with no water and no food. Ekopo's emergency water trucking and fortified porridge reached her village in time. She now dreams of becoming a nurse.
